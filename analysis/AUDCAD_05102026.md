## Critical findings for the EA project

### F1. The macro tape is **not symmetric** - and the master strategy assumes it is

The CSV's symmetric mean-reversion pattern was profitable in the 2024-2025 range era (price oscillating 0.86-0.93). In 2026 the multi-year range broke and the strategy now consistently fights the trend on its short legs. The catastrophic 1/31/2026 "order compression" (`-$43,365.78` cents) and the deep 5/5-5/8 sell basket are both expressions of the same mismatch: symmetric strategy + asymmetric tape.

**EA implication**: even if v1 strictly replicates the master (per `plans/2`), v1.1/v2 must support a **directional bias filter** anchored to D1 EMA20 or W1 EMA20.

### F2. M15 is the right execution timeframe, but the trigger likely combines TFs

Every CSV entry timestamp falls on a 15-minute boundary, and M15 produces clean oscillator extremes at probe-typical price points. The current snapshot has both M15 *and* M5 oversold simultaneously - a configuration that fits the CSV's standalone-probe-win shape (8-18 pips, 30 min - 5 h hold).

**EA implication**: design the trigger as **M15 signal + M5 timing**, not pure single-TF. Cheaper than a multi-indicator combo and matches the 15-minute boundary pattern in the CSV.

### F3. There is a *live, falsifiable test* of the trigger hypothesis right now

At chart capture (2026-05-10, ~13:28 UTC+3), the conditions for an M15/M5 oversold-Stoch-RSI long probe are present. **If the master fires a long probe in the next few M15 bars**, the trigger is almost certainly Stoch-RSI based. **If it does not fire**, the trigger has additional gating (HTF agreement, range-vs-trend filter, time-of-session, news avoidance).

**EA implication**: monitor the live account closely for the next 24h. The presence/absence of a probe at this M5/M15 setup is more informative than 10 historical screenshots.

### F4. The currently-open 5/8 sell basket is on the wrong side of every timeframe

| Open leg | Price | HTF context | M15/M5 context |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5/8 03:00 sell | 0.98458 | Against HTF trend | Against current oversold setup |
| 5/8 09:30 sell | 0.98672 | Against HTF trend | Against current oversold setup |
| 5/8 15:45 sell | 0.99109 | Against HTF trend; near macro resistance | Against current oversold setup |

Weighted-average basket entry ~ **0.98927**; current 0.99088 -> ~16 pips against the basket. The basket needs price to *drop through* an oversold oversold-cross-up setup against an HTF uptrend. That can happen - but every signal in our analysis says the path of least resistance is up.

**EA implication**: `plans/1`'s 20% DD cap and pre-trade ladder fit-check (which the master lacked) would have either (a) refused the 5/8 ladder when it placed the 0.99109 add at macro resistance, or (b) sized it small enough that adverse continuation stays survivable. The 1/31/2026 disaster + this open basket are two data points making the same case for v1's risk gate.

### F5. The grid step (~22 pips) is calibrated to a specific volatility regime

During chop (Asian session), 22 pips = many M5 bars - the grid is comfortably-spaced and adds occur at clear local extremes. During an impulse (5/9 ~15:00 spike: 60 pips in ~2 hours), 22 pips collapses to ~8-10 M5 bars - adds fire too fast and the ladder runs ahead of risk math.

**EA implication**: a `MaxBarRangePips` filter on the prior M5/M15 bar (don't add inside a confirmed impulse candle) is a low-cost insurance policy. Add to the design backlog. Volatility-scaled grid step (`plans/2` D2/Q2) would solve it more cleanly but at the cost of complexity.

## How this fits the project

This is **snapshot 1** of a recurring analysis. The folder `analysis/<DATE>/` will accumulate one set per capture, with `analysis/AUDCAD_<DATE>.md` (this file) as the synthesis. As snapshots accumulate, they become the empirical base that complements the CSV when designing and tuning the EA. We do not write the EA until enough snapshots agree on the structural reads - `plans/1` (mechanics) and `plans/2` (trigger) supply the rules; the analysis snapshots supply the validation that the rules actually fit the tape.
