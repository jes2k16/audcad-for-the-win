## HTF gate analysis

The D1 EMA20 gate was **active** throughout. January started with `gate_short=true`, blocking the first BUY signal:

```
2025.01.02 08:00:00  GATE_BLOCK | LONG | htf_veto_buy
2025.01.02 08:15:00  SIGNAL | SELL → PROBE_OPEN | SHORT | 0.89290
```

**Gate-ON result vs gate-OFF (v4, v1.3 EA)**:

| Config | Gate | Final balance | Net return | Emergencies | BLOCK_ADD |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| v1.3 v4 | OFF | $1,735.84 | +73.58% | 1 (Apr) | 0 |

| Δ | — | +$338.88 | +33.89 pp | −1 | 0 |

The HTF gate eliminated the April 2025 emergency (the AUDCAD crash from ~0.900 to ~0.852 in early April was a strong D1 downtrend, so gate_long=false correctly blocked new LONG probes during that period). Allowing only SHORT probes into the trend produced profitable baskets rather than an adverse counter-trend recovery.

## Negative net-pips closes (16 events)

All 16 are tagged `tp_10.0_pips`. These occur when:
- The basket wavg+10pip TP threshold is met at bar close (check passes)
- The actual close order executes at next bar open with spread/slippage
- The resulting fill is slightly below the target (spread on AUDCADm# is ~2.7 pips)

Multi-leg baskets are most affected (larger spread impact per net pip). Worst case: −15.7 pips on a 3-leg basket (≈ −$8 net on 0.13×39×0.06949). This is expected behavior, not a logic fault.
