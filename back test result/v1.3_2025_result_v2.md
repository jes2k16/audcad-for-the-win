**Reading the run.** From probe to L8 took **28 hours** — eight legs spaced almost evenly through Mar 9–10 as AUDCAD walked 230 pips higher. L9 was correctly blocked by the forward-DD pre-trade check at 07:15 on Mar 11. After that, the basket sat *frozen at L8* while AUDCAD kept moving up against it for another 10 hours, until the on-tick emergency rule fired at DD=20.1% (the close happened at 17:00:27 — note the seconds-precision, this is the tick-driven safety, not a bar-close decision).

This is the exact same failure mode the v1 run produced on three separate occasions. The auto-sizer correctly recomputed `base=0.06` for that basket at `wc_pct=17.82` — within its 20% budget — but the basket *still* hit the emergency cap, because **`FitCheck` budgets to `loss / initial_equity ≤ 20%` while the on-tick emergency compares to `current_equity`**. As loss grows, current_equity shrinks, so the *effective* emergency threshold sits around `initial / 6 ≈ 16.7%` of the probe-time equity. The structural −3.3 pp gap is unchanged across feeds — v1 hit it three times, v2 hit it once.

**The EA is literally identical between the two runs. So what's actually different?**

1. **Spread / pricing model.** XM and Doo source their AUDCAD from different aggregations. Even fractions-of-a-pip differences at probe-fill price compound across 200+ baskets, and they shift where the grid steps fire on tick.
2. **Tick density.** Different brokers replay history with different intra-bar tick patterns. The 22-pip grid step is evaluated on every tick; the *exact tick* where the grid line is touched changes whether a leg adds at 22.0 pips or 22.5 pips of adverse, which changes the weighted average and the eventual TP/emergency math.
3. **Net effect on direction split.** Probes are gated by *D1 close vs D1 EMA20*. The two feeds' daily closes differ enough that v1 generated 142 SHORT permits and v2 generated only 46. The same Feb–May 2026 calendar window produced very different daily-bar OHLC under each provider, because their daily-close timestamps and roll-overs aren't identical.
4. **Consequence.** v1 took 142 SHORT probes into a market its feed showed as more bidirectional. v2 took 46 SHORT probes (mostly the early-March pullback) into a market its feed showed as strongly trending up. SHORTs are the failure direction (a basket fighting an uptrend keeps laddering until it hits the cap). Fewer SHORTs → fewer chances at the blowup mode → better outcome. **Same EA, smaller exposure to the failure mode.**

The key takeaway is **not** "v1.3 actually works after all." The takeaway is **broker-feed selection materially alters the result**, and any single tester pass is partly a sample of the broker's data more than of the strategy. Forward testing on the actual deploy broker (XM cent for the canonical v1.3 target) is the only credible validator.

## 7. Strategy insights — v1.3 specific (post-v2 update)

**1. Same auto-sizing math, same correctness.** `[UNIT_SANITY]`, `[WC_CONST]=44627`, `[AUTOSIZE] base=0.06 wc_pct=19.51` all match the v1.3 §2 derivation. The base auto-stepped 0.06 → 0.05 → 0.06 across the run as equity moved through the floor. *This part of v1.3 needs no change.*

**2. The −3.3 pp structural gap between `FitCheck` (20% of initial) and the on-tick emergency (20% of current) is feed-independent.** It produced 3 blowups in v1, 1 in v2. A single L8 adverse run is enough to trip the gap in either feed — v2 just got fewer chances at it because the gate kept the EA out of SHORTs for 78 of 94 days. *The fix from v1's writeup ([§5 (a)–(c)](v1.3_2025_result.md))* still applies:
- **(a) Soft DD buffer** (`TargetDDPct=15%` for sizing, leave 5pp cushion below emergency).
- **(b) Larger `FitPadPips`** (5 → 20–25) so the formula assumes a deeper-than-grid worst case.
- **(c) Lower `MaxLegs`** (10 → 7 or 8) to shrink the worst-case directly.

**3. Direction asymmetry is huge and feed-dependent.** v1: 107L/142S → blew up SHORT. v2: 159L/46S → survived. The D1 EMA20 gate is doing the directional filtering as designed; the asymmetric outcome reflects asymmetric data, not an EA bug. *Implication:* the trend-detection gate is doing real risk work, but it cannot save a basket once a probe is open. A second filter inside the close-watcher (e.g. "if `gate_short` flips OFF while a SHORT basket is open and basket is at L4+, close at next favorable wavg crossing instead of waiting for +10 pips") would prevent the L4→L8 spiral that the emergency cleans up at maximum cost.

**4. The pip-target close is now strictly profitable.** v1 had 13 baskets where `reason=tp_10.0_pips` but `net_pips < 0` (slippage / spread / gap-fill). v2 has **zero** such baskets — every TP close was genuinely above water. Different feed, different gap behaviour, but worth flagging: the +10 pip target's "looks like profit but isn't" failure mode is real, just feed-dependent.

**5. The strategy works in trending markets when it's on the right side of the trend.** v2 made +$23.5k on LONG L2+ TP closes (47 baskets, mean +$501) — the grid-recovery basket model captures the pullback, the gate keeps it on the trend's side, and the +10 pip target locks small wins consistently. The math is sound. The risk is concentrated in the rare counter-trend basket that escalates to L8+ and hits the FitCheck/emergency gap.

**6. `BLOCK_ADD` worked, but as in v1, blocking new adds doesn't shrink existing exposure.** L9 was correctly refused at 07:15 on Mar 11. The basket sat at L8 for ~10 more hours bleeding into the cap, until emergency fired. *A `TrimBasket` action (proactively close the smallest leg or the worst-positioned leg when forward DD would breach) is a more aggressive but more direct fix than the recommendations above.* Single sentence of intent, several pages of edge cases to make robust.

## 8. What this run actually validates

Treat this run as a **broker-sensitivity diagnostic**, not as a strategy verdict.

- ✓ The EA's `[UNIT_SANITY]`, `[WC_CONST]`, `[AUTOSIZE]` instrumentation is consistent across feeds.
- ✓ The auto-sizer's response to equity changes is correct and prompt.
- ✓ The D1 EMA20 gate does meaningful directional filtering (78/94 days in this feed).
- ✓ The `+10 pip` basket TP is reliably profitable on this feed (0 below-water closes).
- ✗ The structural FitCheck/emergency 3.3 pp gap is **unchanged** and continues to allow individual basket blowups. One L8 SHORT was enough to cost 17.97% of capital.
- ⚠ **The headline result (+8.74%) is feed-dependent.** A different feed, same period, same EA, same inputs produced −18.35%. Do not generalize either number.
