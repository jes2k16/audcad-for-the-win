**Symbol**: AUDCADm# (XM cent/micro)
**Period**: 2023.01.02 – 2023.12.31
**Starting equity**: $1,000 USD
**Final equity**: $838.18 USD
**Net result**: −$161.82 (−16.18%)

---

## What happened in plain English

The EA traded profitably from January through late May — over 200 baskets closed at target. By early June, equity had grown to roughly **$1,500+** (the auto-sizer bumped base from 0.10 up to 0.16, requiring ~$1,506 equity).

Then June 2023 delivered back-to-back disasters within 7 days:

1. **Jun 8 SHORT basket** — AUDCAD rallied ~200 pips over 8 days. The basket hit 8 legs and emergency-exited on **Jun 16** at 35.2% DD (67.36 lots, −77.6 pips).
2. **Jun 19 LONG basket** — opened after partial recovery, immediately caught in a reversal. Hit 8 legs and emergency-exited on **Jun 23** at 35.1% DD (50.52 lots, −76.7 pips).

After the second emergency, equity settled at **$838.18** — below the vol_min sizing floor (~$963). The EA never traded again for the rest of the year.

## Why it stopped trading after June 23

After the second emergency, every signal logged:

```
SKIP_PROBE | LONG | base_below_vol_min eq=838.18
```

The auto-sizer needs roughly **$963 minimum equity** to open a 0.10 lot base at 35% DD cap:

```
min_equity = vol_min × WC_CONST × pv_per_lot / MaxDD%
           = 0.10 × 44,627 × 0.07383 / 0.35
           ≈ $942–963
```

At $838, the computed base would be ~0.08 lots — below the broker minimum of 0.10. Every probe attempt gets skipped. SELL signals were additionally blocked by the HTF gate for much of the rest of the year.

**The account cannot recover on its own.** Same structural issue as 2024.

## Root cause: June 2023 was a perfect storm

2023 H1 saw substantial AUDCAD volatility. June specifically delivered:
- A sharp **bearish-to-bullish reversal** (catching the SHORT grid)
- Followed immediately by a **bullish-to-bearish reversal** (catching the LONG grid)

Both reversals were large enough (~175–200 pips) to build 8-leg baskets before the DD cap fired. The second emergency compounded the first because the account had not recovered enough equity to absorb another L8 event.

The HTF gate (D1 EMA20) was ON and filtering signals — 6,741 gate blocks across the year — but could not prevent mid-basket emergencies (the gate only blocks new probes, not leg additions).

## Key issues

1. **Double-emergency in 7 days** — the second emergency happened before the account had any chance to recover from the first. A mandatory cooling period or reduced lot size after an emergency could help.

2. **Base=0.16 amplified E1 damage** — at peak equity ~$1,506, the 8-leg basket total was 67.36 lots. A tighter DD cap (e.g. 25%) would have triggered earlier with fewer legs and smaller total lot exposure.

3. **Whipsaw = double trap** — June 2023 was particularly adversarial: first a strong rally, then a sharp reversal. Both baskets were caught in trend continuations. A volatility or ATR filter on new probes could detect these regimes.

4. **No recovery path** — same structural issue as 2024: once equity drops below ~$963, the EA is permanently frozen with no built-in restart mechanism.

---

## Verdict

The 2023 run is the worst performance in the multi-year backtest series. Two consecutive L8 emergencies within one week in June destroyed the gains from 5 months of profitable trading and left the account permanently frozen for the remaining 6 months.

This was an extremely unlucky month — back-to-back directional sweeps in opposite directions within days. But "unlucky" events like this will recur. The core risk is structural: the EA has no per-emergency position-size reduction, no cooling-off period, and no equity-floor restart protocol.

**Next step**: Re-run 2023 with `Max_Drawdown_Percentage=20` to measure whether a tighter cap limits per-emergency damage enough to keep equity above the $963 floor after both events.
