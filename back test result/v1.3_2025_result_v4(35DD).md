## 9. My thoughts — what this run proves and doesn't prove

### What this run proves

1. **The Option A config is mechanically sound on the actual broker.** [`UNIT_SANITY`](../EA/AUDCAD_M15_v1_3.mq5#L859-L866), [`WC_CONST`](../EA/AUDCAD_M15_v1_3.mq5#L868-L870), [`AUTOSIZE`](../EA/AUDCAD_M15_v1_3.mq5#L897-L908) all logged the values the math predicted. The auto-sizer correctly picked `base=0.11` (not 0.10), correctly stepped upward as equity grew, and correctly stepped *down* to 0.10 after the emergency loss. The 35% cap fired exactly once, at exactly the 35% line.
2. **The strategy survives a single worst-case loss and resumes compounding.** The April L8 LONG was the canonical bad basket — a 196-pip adverse move in 19 hours. The EA closed it at the cap, downsized, won the next two, and was back to base=0.13 by June. This is the v1.3 §2 promise being kept under live broker conditions on the actual deployment profile.
3. **The cent symbol is correctly identified by the EA.** [`account_type_tag=cent`](../EA/AUDCAD_M15_v1_3.mq5#L842-L843), `contract_size=1000`, `pv_per_lot=0.06949` — all match the CLAUDE.md spec for what XM cent should look like. G10 effectively passes.
4. **Compounding is real and asymmetric in your favor.** Base went 0.11 → 0.19 over the year. The 0.19 baskets at year-end were earning ~1.7× the dollar profit per pip vs the January baskets, on the same +10 pip TP rule. That's the engine that turned a +25% raw signal edge into +73% account growth.

### What this run does NOT prove

1. **It's still one year, one broker, one realization.** v3 saw +48% on standard. v4 saw +73% on cent. v1 saw -18%. The variance across (broker, year) pairs is large. A +73% year is not an expected value; it's a sample.
2. **35% DD is genuinely dangerous in a 2-σ regime.** The April emergency was a normal-vol event (196 pips in 19 hours on AUDCAD is unusual but not extreme). A real stress event — Sunday gap, central bank surprise, liquidity hole — produces 300+ pip moves. At base=0.19 with 35% cap, a forced emergency exit costs ~$590 = 34% of the late-year equity. Two such events in a row would near-zero the account from $1.7k → $750.
3. **Gate-off increased trade density but also removed the directional safety filter.** v3 with gate on: 377 probes / year, 0 emergencies. v4 with gate off: 549 probes / year, 1 emergency. The gate's HTF veto was doing real work. We don't yet know whether the April emergency would have been *gated out* — that's a re-run worth doing.
4. **22 negative-`tp_10.0` closes total -103 pips.** Small in dollar terms (most happened at low base), but the pattern is structural: M15 bar close logic + tick-based exit can disagree. This is a known v1.2 issue carried into v1.3. Worth a separate fix at some point.

### What I would do next

In priority order:

1. **Re-run v4 with `EnableGate=true` (everything else identical).** This isolates the gate's contribution at the cent/$1k profile. Hypothesis: probe count drops to ~370, the April basket is gated out, final balance is *lower* (you give up volume) but `emergency_dd` count is *0*. If that's the result, the gate is buying real insurance for measurable yield.
2. **Re-run v4 with `MaxDDPct=30` instead of 35 (gate off).** This narrows the cap to the absolute minimum that still admits `base=0.10` (`0.10 × 44627 × 0.06949 / 1000 = 31.01%` — would just barely fit). Tests whether the April emergency would have fired *earlier* (smaller dollar loss) at the cost of slightly fewer trades qualifying.
3. **Re-run v4 on a different broker's cent feed (if available).** The +73% number is a Doo-style observation on XM's feed. A different XM-cent broker (or even a different account) tests how feed-dependent this result is.
4. **G12 formally passes** if you accept the 35% cap as the spec — but the original v1.3 spec said 20%. So G12 is *open* until you either (a) accept the 35% spec change in writing, or (b) demonstrate that some other config (smaller `MaxLegs`, larger `GridStepPips`, etc.) keeps the 20% cap and still trades at $1k cent.
5. **Update CLAUDE.md G11 prediction.** Current text says `base=0.06, wc_pct≈18.7` at $1k. That assumed `vol_min=0.01` which is wrong for XM cent. The correct prediction is: *"$1k cent with stock 20% cap will skip every probe; minimum-trading equity ≈ $1,643. With `MaxDDPct=35` override, base auto-picks 0.11 at probe-open."*

### One-line verdict

**Option A delivered +73.58% on $1k cent over 2025 with one absorbed emergency — the math, the auto-sizer, and the recovery all worked as specified. But you bought that +73% by raising tail-risk acceptance from 20% to 35% and removing the HTF directional filter. The next experiment — same config with the gate back on — will tell you how much of that yield was the relaxed cap and how much was just the density boost.**
