**1. The edge is the ladder, not the signal.** Level-1 probes (0.01 lot) won 194/202 of the time but produced only +$151.76 all year — the 0.01 lot is too small to matter. **98% of the year's profit came from the 152 baskets that laddered.** The strategy is, in effect, a martingale grid: the signal just picks a direction; the money is made when price runs adverse, the EA piles in 24×/36×/48×… lots, and the whole basket mean-reverts +10 pips on a now-large weighted size.

**2. 100% basket win rate — but that is the martingale signature, not proof of safety.** Every one of the 354 closed baskets hit its +10-pip target; nothing was stopped out and the 20% DD cap never fired. That is exactly what a grid-recovery system looks like *while the market keeps mean-reverting*. The risk is not in the win rate — it is in the one basket that does not come back.

**3. Tail risk is real and was approached.** The deepest basket reached **level 8 (4.21 lots on a $50k account)** before recovering (#215, +$516.92). A level-10 basket would carry **6.49 lots** (~$45/pip). The ladder works until it doesn't — 2025 simply never produced a move deep and sustained enough to run a basket to MaxLegs or trip the DD cap. One trending year against the basket direction is the scenario this backtest does not contain.

**4. The `tp` exit can still close underwater — a genuine bug surface.** **16 baskets** closed with *negative* net_pips even though the close reason was `tp_10.0_pips`. `CheckCloseTarget()` decides on the **last closed M15 bar** (`iClose(...,1)`), but `CloseBasket()` executes at the **current bid/ask**. When there is a gap between the trigger bar and execution — notably weekend gaps (5 of the 16 bad closes executed on a Monday) — the realised price can be well past the target. Worst case: **#117, a level-3 short, logged `tp_10.0_pips` but realised −16.5 pips (≈ −$68)**. Worth fixing: re-check the live price inside `CheckCloseTarget` before committing the close, or skip the very first M15 bar after a session gap.

**5. The D1 EMA20 gate is doing heavy filtering.** `GATE_BLOCK` fired ~3,535 times against only 355 probes opened — the HTF gate vetoed roughly 10 signals for every one it let through. Combined with the v1.2 single-basket rule, the EA is in the market far less than the raw signal would suggest. That is good for tail risk but means the result leans heavily on the gate's direction call being right.

**6. Direction-balanced, but longs carried the year.** 175 long / 179 short baskets — well balanced — yet longs returned +$4,203.73 vs shorts +$2,364.64. AUDCAD spent much of 2025 grinding higher, so long ladders recovered faster and shallower while short ladders had to fight the drift. A genuinely trending year is when this asymmetry would turn negative.

**7. Open-ended baskets tie up the account.** Median hold was 7h but the longest laddered baskets ran **9–12 days** (e.g. #345 ran 2025.11.25 → 12.05). Because v1.2 is single-basket-at-a-time, a deep basket that sits underwater for over a week blocks every other signal in that window — visible in the monthly counts (December opened only 9 probes, partly because late-year baskets ran long).

### Bottom line

v1.2 returned **+13.1% in 2025 with a 100% basket win rate and zero stop-outs** — a clean result, but a *characteristically martingale* one. The backtest confirms the mechanics work in a mean-reverting / mildly-trending year; it does **not** test the failure mode (a sustained trend against basket direction running the ladder to level 10 / the DD cap). Before any live deployment: (a) fix the gap-driven `tp`-but-negative close, (b) stress-test against a deliberately adverse trending period, and (c) confirm the level-8→10 lot sizes are survivable on the intended account size.
