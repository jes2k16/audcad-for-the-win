## 6. My thoughts about the strategy

You asked for the assessment. Here's where I land after digesting all three runs.

### What this strategy is actually doing

v1.3 is a **mean-reversion grid-recovery basket** with an **equity-adaptive sizer** sitting under a **trend-direction veto gate**. Translated:

- The **signal** (RSI + StochRSI + BB%B + swing-distance) picks the *side* of a potential mean reversion at the M15 close.
- The **D1 EMA20 gate** vetoes signals fighting the daily trend — it's the "don't catch falling knives" filter.
- If the first probe goes adverse, the **22-pip grid** averages down with a 12-N multiplier ladder — so the weighted-average price moves toward current price *fast*, letting a small reversion close the whole basket at +10 net pips.
- The **auto-sizer** computes the probe size so that a worst-case 10-leg basket (44,627 lot-pips) consumes exactly 20% of *current* equity. As equity grows, the probe grows; as equity shrinks, the probe shrinks.

What v1.3 is **not** doing: prediction. The signal fires hundreds of times more often than the actual master account ever did. The strategy is *not* trying to predict reversions — it's trying to *survive* until the next one and let the basket math do the work.

### What the three runs prove and don't prove

**Proven:**

1. **Mechanics work as specified.** `[UNIT_SANITY]`, `[WC_CONST]`, `[AUTOSIZE]` all match the spec in all three runs. The auto-sizer scales correctly both up (v3: 0.06→0.09 on equity growth) and down (v1: 0.06→0.04 on equity loss). The +10 pip TP fires when expected. `BLOCK_ADD` correctly refuses overflow legs.
2. **In a normal bidirectional year, the strategy is highly profitable.** +48% on $100k in 12 months with zero blowups, every month positive, ladder depth distribution well within the safety budget. The L8 SHORT save in v3 demonstrates the deep-ladder case works when the regime delivers a reversion.
3. **The structural FitCheck-vs-emergency 3.3 pp gap is feed-independent.** It manifested in v1 (3 blowups) and v2 (1 blowup), and did not manifest in v3 (because no basket got far enough into adverse territory to test it). The gap is real and the fixes from [v2 §7](v1.3_2025_result_v2.md) still apply — they would buy insurance, not central-tendency lift.

**Not proven:**

1. **Out-of-sample expectancy.** Three runs are not three independent samples. The v3 2025 result is one realization of one year's data on one broker. The +48% number is a *single observation*, not an expected value. To trust the central tendency you'd want 5+ years on the same broker, then a different broker, then live shadow.
2. **The cent-profile target.** Every single backtest here is on a standard symbol. G10/G11/G12 (the canonical $1k cent-account tests) are still unrun. v1.3's whole *point* is to be deployable from $1k of real capital, and we have zero data on that exact configuration.
3. **Behaviour in a 2-σ stress year.** 2025 was friendly. 2026 YTD was unfriendly. Neither is the "broker default goes dark, AUDCAD gaps 200 pips on a Sunday open" stress test that costs ladder strategies their accounts. The 20% cap is the only stress backstop, and it has a known 3.3 pp gap.

### Where the strategy is durable

- **The math is right.** Auto-sizing closes the loop between equity and exposure. Most retail grid systems leave this open and blow up when equity grows but lot size doesn't.
- **The D1 gate is doing real work.** In v2, 78/94 days `gate_long`; the gate kept the EA out of the trending direction's wrong side most of the time. The asymmetric outcomes across runs largely reflect the gate doing what it was designed for.
- **The +10 pip TP is conservative and reliable.** No "tp but actually negative" closes in v2 or v3 (13 in v1, all small). The basket math means a TP fire usually clears spread + commission with room to spare.
- **The signal is intentionally over-firing.** Hundreds of probes per year ≠ overtrading. The strategy needs density so the basket has many shots at being in the right place when the next reversion hits. Probe wins are small (+$1.5k from 228 L1-only baskets in v3); the money comes from the L2+ ladder closes (+$51k from 148 baskets). That's a feature.

### Where the strategy is fragile

1. **One bad basket can erase a quarter of profit.** The v1 emergency baskets each lost ~$19k. On $100k equity, that's ~19% — almost a full year's worth of normal-regime gains. The 20% cap is brittle: the FitCheck is calibrated to 20% of *initial* equity but the on-tick emergency fires at 20% of *current* equity, so a basket that fits at probe-time can still trip the emergency. **You will not feel safe sized at the cap.**
2. **Standard-account math is a 6× exposure multiplier vs the master.** The master account used 0.05 cent (≈ 0.0005 standard) for its probes. v1.3 at base=0.06 standard is *120× the master per-probe exposure*. The strategy is reverse-engineered from a cent-account master and then deployed on a standard account where every probe carries dramatically more dollar risk. The whole reason v1.3 added the cent default and `RequireCentAccount` was to acknowledge this. The cent-profile tests still need to actually run.
3. **Broker-feed variance is large enough to flip the sign.** v1 −18.35% vs v2 +8.74% on the same period. That's a 27-percentage-point spread driven entirely by which broker's price feed you backtested against. Live results will depend on the *deploy* broker's feed, not on whichever feed gave the best backtest.
4. **The signal density × no-skip-on-trend-extension means the EA is permanently exposed in trending markets.** When AUDCAD trends 800 pips in 4 months (v2's feed), the EA *will* eventually open a counter-trend SHORT during a pullback that doesn't follow through. That's not a bug — it's the design — but the cost in trending years is bounded only by the 20% cap, which has the 3.3 pp gap.

### What I would do before live deployment

Rough priority, what I think actually matters:

1. **Run G10/G11/G12 (the cent-profile tests).** This is the strategy's stated target. Everything to date is diagnostic. If `AUDCAD.c` `contract_size` is not 1000 on the broker, none of these standard-account numbers translate. *This is the highest-information next experiment.*
2. **Implement `TargetDDPct=15` (separate from `MaxDDPct=20`).** Size the ladder to 15% worst case, leave a 5 pp cushion. Single-line change in `ComputeBaseLot()`. This would have flipped at least one of v1's three blowups into a survivable basket. On v3 (where DD wasn't reached) it would cost ~25% of profit (smaller ladders → smaller wins) but cap the catastrophic case. **Trade central-tendency yield for tail safety.**
3. **Run v3-equivalent (2025 full year) on the v1 broker (XMGlobal-MT5 9) — and on at least one third broker.** Three (broker, year=2025) datapoints will tell you whether the +48% is a Doo artifact or a strategy property. If two of three brokers give >+20% on 2025 with zero blowups, the strategy has real signal. If only Doo does, the +48% is a feed quirk.
4. **Live shadow on cent demo for 4 weeks minimum** (not 2 — 4). `Shadow=true`, log every signal vs. master, compare. This is the only thing that will tell you whether the gate, sizer, and TP logic survive *live* spread and execution latency. Backtests can't.
5. **If 1–4 all pass, deploy with `TargetDDPct=15` and **half** the recommended capital** (so a worst-case blowup costs the budget you can afford to lose, not the budget the math says is safe).

### One-line verdict

**v1.3 is a working strategy that thrives in bidirectional regimes and has a known, fixable tail-risk gap. The 2025 +48% result is genuine, but it is one observation, and the strategy's deployable safety margin is structurally thinner than its specification implies until the FitCheck/emergency gap is closed.**

I would treat this as a "promising candidate worth running cent-profile tests on", not a "ready to deploy" result. The next experiment is G10/G11, not a bigger backtest.
