## 3. What we know about entries - combined view

### From the CSV alone

1. **Timeframe**: every entry timestamp falls on a 15-minute boundary (`:00, :15, :30, :45`). The master decides on **M15 bar close** (or coarser, but never finer).
2. **Bidirectional, flip-prone**: probes alternate buy/sell within hours.
3. **Probe-win shape**: standalone probes that don't escalate close in **30 min - 5 h for +8 to +18 pips** - tight TP, mean-reversion-like.
4. **Probes cluster at local extremes**: highs trigger sells, lows trigger buys (e.g., 5/4 same-day sell @ 0.97985 *and* buy @ 0.97575).
5. **Symmetric opposing baskets coexist**: long and short baskets can be alive simultaneously.
6. **No weekend entries**.
7. **Re-arming after close**: a fresh probe (often opposite direction) appears within hours of basket close.
8. **Grid-add cadence is price-based, not time-based** (~22 pip step) - already locked in `plans/1`. The trigger we're solving here is **probe entry only**.

### From the multi-TF snapshot (2026-05-10)

9. **The 2026 tape is asymmetric.** AUDCAD broke its multi-year 0.86-0.95 range and is now testing 0.99 macro resistance with all HTFs (M1, W1, D1, H4) bullish. Symmetric mean-reversion that worked in 2024-2025 is structurally biased to lose on its short legs in this regime.
10. **M15 + M5 momentum often disagrees with HTF direction** - exactly the setup a single-TF mean-reversion trigger fades. This is why standalone probes win quickly (8-18 pips of M15 unwind) but escalated baskets blow up (HTF reasserts itself for 100+ pips).
11. **M5 oscillators alone are too noisy** to be the decision timeframe - but ideal for execution-timing within an M15 signal.
12. **Two open shorts (5/8 ladder, weighted ~ 0.98927) are currently against every timeframe.** The master's symmetric trigger placed them anyway. Confirms the absence of an HTF filter in the source strategy.

### Combined inference

**Strongest prior**: the master uses a **mean-reversion oscillator on M15** (RSI, Stoch RSI, or BB-touch), with execution alignable to M5 close. **No HTF filter** - that's the source of the strategy's drawdown profile, and the *reason* the 20% DD cap exists in `plans/1`.

## 7. Hypothesis Evaluation Loop

For each hypothesis H1-H7, mark each of the 10 historic probes (and any live probes from Section 5) as **PASS** or **FAIL**.

- **PASS** = the rule would have fired *in the correct direction* on that bar (or the bar immediately preceding the entry).
- **FAIL** = the rule would not have fired, or would have fired in the wrong direction.

Rules:
1. A hypothesis is **eliminated** on first FAIL.
2. Survivors are ranked by:
   - **Simplicity** (fewer indicators, fewer parameters wins).
   - **Operational ease** (cleanly expressible in MQL5 with built-in indicators).
   - **Robustness** (no parameter that needs to change between samples).
3. If multiple hypotheses survive cleanly, prefer the simplest. If H1 and H3 both survive, prefer **H3** if H3 explains the live test and H1 doesn't (conditional rank).
4. If none survive, see Section 10 D1.

### 13.Y - Strategy recommendation (working draft, replaces Section 8's provisional rule)

Based on the 11-week walkthrough plus the 0510 multi-TF snapshot:

**Probe trigger (sell)** - fires on M15 bar close when **all** of:
1. **Stoch RSI(14, 14, 3, 3) %K crosses down from >= 80** on M15, and **%K < %D**.
2. **M5 close in the next bar confirms** (M5 close < M15 close at the trigger bar).

**Probe trigger (buy)** - mirror of the above (%K crosses up from <= 20, %K > %D, M5 close > M15 close).

- Price has moved >= `GridStepPips` (default 22) from the prior basket leg's price, on an M15 boundary.
- **AND** `MaxBarRangePipsM5/M15` not exceeded (Week 7 gating).
- **AND** spread <= `MaxSpreadPips`.
- Size of the new leg: probe = 1x `BaseLot`; first add = `FirstAddMultiplier` (default 24x); subsequent adds add `LinearAddIncrement` (default +12x per add).

**Basket close** - fires when:
- Basket weighted-average price reaches the favorable side of the current market price by `BasketTPPips` (default 6 pips of net positive), **and** the oscillator that triggered the probe has flipped back out of its extreme.
- **OR** the 20% DD cap from `plans/1` triggers.

**Runner-up rule** - **H1 (BB touch)** kept as a fallback `Input` (default off). Switching is a one-line config change for A/B testing in v1.1.

- **v1.1**: A/B between H3 and H1 by config.

#### What the additional constraint likely is

The master fires ~1x/day from a pool of ~75 qualifying bars/day. The missing 70x selectivity most likely comes from one (or more) of:

1. **Basket concurrency constraint** (most likely): master does not open a same-direction probe while a same-direction basket is already active. Since each basket runs for hours to days, this suppresses the effective probe rate dramatically. Test: load full history, compute basket open/close windows, apply concurrency filter, recompute precision.

2. **Intra-bar price-action event** (possible): a specific M15 or M5 candlestick pattern (pin bar, engulfing) within the zone triggers the actual entry. Not measurable from OHLC close-level data alone.

3. **HTF level test** (less likely -- already excluded by Section 13.2/13.8): H1/H4 support or resistance test at the probe time. But the 91% recall from M15-only data means HTF cannot be primary.

4. **Time-of-day filter** (not supported by data): hourly firing profile is uniform (110-150 signals per hour, all 24 hours). Master's actual probes don't show strong hour concentration either.

- Use Section 14.9b as a "green light" (zone active = scanning mode)
- Add basket-state gate: skip signal if same-direction basket is already open (test in G5/G6 script)
- If basket-gate alone doesn't explain selectivity: investigate M5 trigger within M15 zone (requires M5 OHLC data from user)
